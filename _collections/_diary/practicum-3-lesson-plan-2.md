<p style="font-size:0.8rem; color:#888;">(R = remembering, U = understanding, A = applying, An = analysing, E = evaluating, C = creating)</p>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>

<p>Board (white/black), chalk/marker, duster.</p>

<h4>3. Questioning</h4>

<p>The teacher will pose some questions, such as:</p>

<ul><li>If you lose your eyesight, how would you feel?</li><li>Have you ever had a conversation with a blind person?</li><li>What do you think — how do blind people see/imagine the world?</li></ul>

<p>To bring the students towards the topic of the lesson, a poem about “The Blind Boy” was used.</p>

<h4>4. Poem Reading</h4>

<p>The teacher will read the poem “The Blind Boy” aloud once or twice, with correct pronunciation, and instruct the students to listen carefully. Next, the teacher will give the students some time to read and underline new words in the poem. Finally, the teacher, with the students' help, will try to infer the meanings of the new words and explain each stanza of the poem.</p>

<h4>5. Assessment</h4>

<p>Once the students are familiar with the new words, the teacher will give them a few minutes to write the main idea of the poem “The Blind Boy,” and then ask them to read it aloud.</p>
