<p><strong>Students will be able to:</strong></p><ul><li>explain the study domain of quantum physics.</li><li>analyse a few natural phenomena through the lens of quantum physics.</li></ul>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>

<p>Board, chalk, duster, projector, speakers, a flower, a burnt paper, and a chocolate.</p>

<h4>3. Motivational Activity (1 minute)</h4>

<p>The class starts with a short video clip showing natural phenomena that can be explained using quantum physics.</p>

<h4>4. Introductory Activity (5 minutes)</h4>

<p>Students listen to the teacher explain the concept of quantum physics by relating it to the video they just watched.</p>

<h4>5. Quantum Effects in Our Surroundings (7 minutes)</h4>

<ul><li>One of the teachers hands students a flower, burnt paper, and chocolate.</li><li>Students are asked to smell these things.</li><li>Students are asked how their tiny nose can smell such a multitude of distinct fragrances around them.</li><li>The teacher explains how quantum physics is at work whenever we smell something.</li></ul>

<h4>6. Assessment Activity (2 minutes)</h4>

<p>The teachers ask questions during teaching, or at the end, that require students to apply their understanding of quantum physics to explain a specific phenomenon.</p>
