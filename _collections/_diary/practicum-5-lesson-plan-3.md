<h4>1. Student Learning Outcomes</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Explain the study domain of quantum physics.</li>
  <li>Analyse a few natural phenomena through the lens of quantum physics.</li>
</ul>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>
<p>Board, chalk, duster, projector, speakers, a flower, a burnt paper and a chocolate</p>

<h4>3. Motivational Activity (1 minute)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>The class would start with a short video showing some natural phenomena that can be explained using quantum physics.</li>
</ul>

<h4>4. Introductory Activity (5 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Students will listen to the teacher.</li>
  <li>He/she will explain the concept of quantum physics by relating it to the video they watched earlier.</li>
</ul>

<h4>5. Quantum Effects in Our Surroundings (7 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>One of the teachers will hand students a flower, burnt paper and chocolate.</li>
  <li>The students will be asked to smell these things.</li>
  <li>Students will be asked, how come their tiny nose can smell such a multitude of distinct fragrances around them.</li>
  <li>Teacher will explain how quantum physics is at work whenever we smell something.</li>
</ul>

<h4>6. Assessment Activity (2 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>The teachers will ask some questions during teaching /at last, that require students to apply their understanding of quantum physics to explain a specific phenomenon.</li>
</ul>
