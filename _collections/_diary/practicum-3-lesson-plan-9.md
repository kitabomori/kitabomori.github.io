<h4>1. Learning Outcomes</h4>

<p><strong>Students will be able to:</strong></p><ul><li><strong>I.</strong> define at least five new words correctly in written and oral sentences <em>(30%)</em></li><li><strong>II.</strong> interpret the meaning of words in a reading passage <em>(35%)</em></li><li><strong>III.</strong> identify synonyms and antonyms for at least three new words <em>(35%)</em></li></ul>

<p style="font-size:0.8rem; color:#888;">(R = remembering, U = understanding, A = applying, An = analysing, E = evaluating, C = creating)</p>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>

<p>Board (white/black), chalk/marker, duster.</p>

<h4>5E Model</h4>

<p><strong>Engagement:</strong> Divide the class into small groups of 3&ndash;4 students each. Give each group a word and ask them to guess what this word might relate to.</p>

<p><strong>Exploration:</strong> Give each group five new words. Instruct the students to write their vocabulary word in the centre of the paper and draw a circle around it, then brainstorm related words or concepts &mdash; synonyms, antonyms, examples, or related ideas.</p>

<p><strong>Explanation:</strong> Write the listed vocabulary words on the board. Ask each group or student to explain the meaning of their chosen vocabulary word, then encourage groups to share their analogies with the class and discuss how they help clarify the meaning of the new words.</p>

<p><strong>Elaboration:</strong> Give each group a vocabulary word and a worksheet asking for synonyms and antonyms, example sentences, and related words or concepts. Students work independently or in small groups to complete it.</p>

<p><strong>Evaluation:</strong> Once the worksheets are complete, have each student share their findings with the class and lead a brief discussion about their word.</p>
