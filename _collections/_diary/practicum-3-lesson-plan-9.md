<h4>2. Required Material</h4>
<p>Board (white/black), chalk/marker, duster</p>

<table style="border-collapse:collapse; margin:1rem 0; font-size:0.95rem; width:100%; border:1px solid #333;">
  <tr>
    <th style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; background:#f5f5f5; text-align:center; font-weight:600;">Engagement</th>
    <td style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; vertical-align:top;">
      <ul>
        <li>Divide the class into small groups of 3-4 students each.</li>
        <li>Give each group a word and ask them to guess what this word might relate to?</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <th style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; background:#f5f5f5; text-align:center; font-weight:600;">Exploration</th>
    <td style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; vertical-align:top;">
      <ul>
        <li>Give the group five new words.</li>
        <li>Instruct the students to write their vocabulary word in the center of the paper and draw a circle around it.</li>
        <li>Next, ask the students to brainstorm related words or concepts associated with their vocabulary words. These could be synonyms, antonyms, examples, or related ideas.</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <th style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; background:#f5f5f5; text-align:center; font-weight:600;">Explanation</th>
    <td style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; vertical-align:top;">
      <ul>
        <li>Write the listed vocabulary words on the board.</li>
        <li>Then, ask each group or student to come up with its meaning and explain the meaning of the chosen vocabulary word.</li>
        <li>Encourage the groups to share their analogies with the class, and discuss how the analogies help to clarify the meaning of the new vocabulary words.</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <th style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; background:#f5f5f5; text-align:center; font-weight:600;">Elaboration</th>
    <td style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; vertical-align:top;">
      <ul>
        <li>Ask each group a vocabulary word and a worksheet that includes the following questions:</li>
        <ul>
          <li>What are some synonyms and antonyms for the word?</li>
          <li>What are some examples of how the word can be used in a sentence?</li>
          <li>What related words or concepts are associated with the word?</li>
        </ul>
        <li>Have students work independently or in small groups to answer the questions and complete the worksheet.</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <th style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; background:#f5f5f5; text-align:center; font-weight:600;">Evaluation</th>
    <td style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; vertical-align:top;">
      <ul>
        <li>Once the worksheets are complete, each student shares their findings with the class and leads a brief discussion about their word.</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
</table>
