<p>On January 12, we conducted the last school visit at the Government X in Old Sukkur. The objective of being in the field was to understand the building, its causes, several of its forms, and to highlight it in the context of the school. The observation sheet provided helped to gather data from the school premises.</p>

<p>Some define bullying as a notorious act where peers and seniors repeatedly or habitually use verbal or physical strength to harm others emotionally and physically. Bullying leads to a serious impact on the personality of both bully and bullied. In the long run, the bully becomes an oppressor, violating norms and laws, while the bullied faces severe psychological problems. The present bullying leads to isolation, depression, and low self-esteem, and at times leads to tampering. In some extreme cases, individuals think about taking their lives. Various factors lead to bullying, some are race, religion, social class, sexual orientation, appearance, physical or mental strength, size, age, gender, character, etc. Some of the types of bullying are verbal, physical, sexual and cyber, etc. Bullies either operate individually or in groups. Some people derive pleasure from inflicting suffering and humiliation on others. Sadism is a concept that describes a behaviour in which people derive satisfaction from making fun of others. At times, victims of bullying also become bullies for vengeance. Most bullying at the school level occurs in the classroom, during break time, and in the playing and recreational areas. Now, bullying is also being reported on social media and online platforms in the shape of ugly text and images. The tragedy of bullying is that it happens all the time around us, directly or indirectly, and to counter it, it needs huge efforts from individuals, from the roots, to take action to stop it in the first place.</p>

<p>Allegedly, in the premises of a school, it is the prime responsibility of a teacher to know what is going on with a particular student. The pale faces of students at times indicate that someone is bullying and victimising them. A teacher should talk to students personally, assure them that they can be a source of comfort, and that the teacher will resolve the problem.</p>
