<p><em>Reflection on Lessons VIII and IX</em></p>

<p>During the ninth visit to Government High School No. 1, Sukkur, I observed three important aspects of teaching and learning:</p>

<ol><li>There should not be a greater gap between one lesson and the next.</li><li>Students need time in class to process the information before moving on to the next topic.</li><li>Active learning techniques are effective in keeping students engaged and promoting deeper understanding.</li></ol>

<p>Firstly, I noticed that the four-night gap between the last lesson and today's class necessitated revisiting the previous lesson. This gap gave students time to forget the previously taught information. It is important to review the previous lesson at the beginning of each class. This can help students recall the material and build on it, rather than starting from scratch every class. At the same time, revising a topic completely takes a lot of time.</p>

<p>Secondly, I realised that students need time to process information before moving on to the next topic. It is essential to ensure that students have understood the material before moving on to a new lesson. Students learn differently, and some may need more time to understand the concepts. Therefore, it is important to encourage students to ask questions and provide them with extra time to reflect and process the information before moving on. By doing so, students will be more confident in their learning and better able to engage with the new material.</p>

<p>Lastly, I observed that active learning techniques are effective in keeping students engaged and promoting deeper understanding. Incorporating activities such as group work, discussions, and debates can help students engage with the material, leading to a deeper understanding of the concepts. Active learning also makes the learning process more enjoyable and reduces the chance of boredom and disengagement. In addition, it allows students to learn from one another and promotes collaboration and teamwork, which are important skills for life beyond the classroom.</p>

<p>In conclusion, teaching is a continuous learning process, and today's lesson helped me to recognise the importance of reviewing the previous lesson, providing students with adequate time to process information, and incorporating active learning techniques to keep students engaged and promote deeper understanding. As a teacher, I will strive to adopt these aspects into my teaching practices and provide my students with the best possible learning experience.</p>
