<h4>1. Learning Outcomes</h4>

<p><strong>Students will be able to:</strong></p><ul><li><strong>I.</strong> identify the subject and verb <em>(30%)</em></li><li><strong>II.</strong> construct simple sentences <em>(40%)</em></li><li><strong>III.</strong> use appropriate punctuation marks in sentences <em>(30%)</em></li></ul>

<p style="font-size:0.85rem; color:#666;">(R = remembering, U = understanding, A = applying, An = analysing, E = evaluating, C = creating)</p>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>

<p>Board (white/black), chalk/marker, duster.</p>

<h4>5E Model</h4>

<p><strong>Engagement:</strong> Start the class by asking students to share their favourite things about school. Then write a few sentences on the board to set an example for the students.</p>

<p><strong>Exploration:</strong> Divide students into pairs and give them a few sentences — simple, compound, and complex — to analyse. Students should identify the subject and verb, then share their findings with the rest of the class.</p>

<p><strong>Explanation:</strong> Write the definitions of sentence, subject, and verb on the board, and explain the differences between the subject and the verb with examples.</p>

<p><strong>Elaboration:</strong> Give students a few words to construct sentences. Allow them to work in pairs or groups to complete the exercises, then share their sentences with the class, highlighting the subject and verb.</p>

<p><strong>Evaluation:</strong> Give students a short quiz to assess their understanding of sentence construction, subject, verb, and punctuation use.</p>
