<h4>1. General Objectives</h4>

<p>Students will be able to:</p>

<ul><li>develop mathematical curiosity when solving problems.</li><li>become confident in using mathematics to analyse and solve problems both in school and in real-life situations.</li><li>develop the knowledge, skills, and attitudes necessary to pursue further studies in mathematics.</li></ul>

<h4>2. Specific Objective (SLOs)</h4>

<p>Students will be able to:</p>

<ul><li>recall what a triangle is.</li><li>differentiate between types by sides: scalene, isosceles, and equilateral.</li><li>locate some examples of triangles from daily life.</li></ul>

<h4>3. Instructional Aids</h4>

<p>Blackboard, chalks, a textbook, triangles made of charts, and a scale.</p>

<h4>4. Motivational Activity</h4>

<p>To grab students' attention, the teacher would ask a student to come to the board. The teacher will then draw three random lines on the board and ask the student to draw a shape of their liking. If the student draws a triangle, the teacher will ask the following questions:</p>

<ul><li>How many sides are in this shape? (Answer: 3)</li><li>How many angles are in this shape? (Answer: 3)</li><li>What is this shape called? (Answer: A triangle)</li></ul>

<p>Announcement &mdash; today, we are going to learn about triangles.</p>

<h4>5. Supervised Study</h4>

<p>Before class, prepare some flashcards with different triangles. The teacher will distribute the triangles to the class and ask students to measure the lengths of the triangles' sides.</p>

<h4>6. Assessment</h4>

<p>The teacher would summarise the whole class by asking different questions to the students, such as:</p>

<ul><li>Tell me what a triangle is.</li><li>What is the name of the triangle which has three different sides?</li><li>What is the name of the triangle which has two equal sides?</li><li>What is the name of the triangle which has three equal sides?</li></ul>
