<h3>4. KWL Plan</h3>

<table style="border-collapse:collapse; margin:1rem 0; font-size:0.95rem; width:100%;">
<tr><th style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; width:33%;">Know<br><small><em>(Previous Knowledge)</em></small></th><th style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; width:33%;">Want to Know<br><small><em>(Teaching Strategy)</em></small></th><th style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; width:33%;">Learned<br><small><em>(Assessment)</em></small></th></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">Begin the lesson by engaging students in a brief discussion about their current reading knowledge.
<ul>
<li>What types of reading do you enjoy?</li>
<li>How do you usually read? (silently, out loud, in groups)</li>
<li>Have you ever heard of or tried loud reading before?</li>
</ul>
</td><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">Facilitate a class discussion to find out what students are curious about regarding reading. Some possible questions include:
<ul>
<li>What questions do you have about reading out loud?</li>
<li>Why might people choose to read out loud?</li>
<li>Are there specific benefits you're curious about?</li>
</ul>
</td><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">Ask the student about the importance of loud reading
<ul>
<li>Reading out loud can enhance comprehension by providing a different sensory input.</li>
<li>Loud reading can improve fluency and oral communication skills.</li>
<li>Expressive reading with proper tone and pronunciation adds depth to the understanding of the text.</li>
<li>Students may discover personal preferences for reading styles, recognising the benefits of loud reading.</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>
</table>
