<div style="text-align:right; font-size:0.85rem; color:#666; margin:0 0 1rem 0;">November 3, 2021</div>

<p>This time around, our task was to interview our cooperative teacher. After reviewing the observation sheet, my colleagues and I agreed on a few questions the teacher asked. From the cooperative teacher's reaction when we asked him about his previous time, he seemed to be trying to escape the interview. But, under continuous pressure, he reluctantly agreed to give some of his valuable time to our learning. The interview focused on classroom management.</p>

<p>The questions were fairly simple, but the responses were fragmented, and only a handful of the information addressed the questions. There was this question: "What are your expectations from the students for a lesson and from the end of the academic year?" The straightforward goal was "to perform better", by which I suspect he was only focusing on the students to make them capable of just solving an exam paper. But what about skill development and character building? The other questions were related to the lesson plan, layout, managing behavioural problems, and students' participation in class. When the teacher answered the lesson question, he said, "It sounds good to plan your lesson, but in reality, it is of no use! I have enough experience to come to class and start teaching." In response to remaining questions, he blamed the previous teachers for not working on students' behaviour and the basics, which, he said, is causing problems for him and other teachers. The teacher did not mention straightforward strategies to address the problems.</p>

<p>Based on the irritation with this particular cooperative teacher, I learned that teaching at this school has been stuck in the past. The teacher is using methods, strategies, or early rights to teach the 21st-century mobile phone generation. The profession of teaching demands that teachers adopt current tactics and teaching styles to manage students in the current era. This experience would be a reminder for me to be an updated teacher who never stops learning how to teach.</p>
