<h4>4. 5E Plan</h4>

<table style="border-collapse:collapse; margin:1rem 0; font-size:0.95rem; width:100%;">
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; font-weight:600;">Engagement</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">
<ul>
<li>Begin with a brief discussion about the concept of free writing.</li>
<li>Ask students to create a mind map in their notebooks, brainstorming ideas related to free writing.</li>
<li>Encourage them to think about what comes to their minds when they hear the term "free writing".</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; font-weight:600;">Exploration</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">
<ul>
<li>Definition: the act of writing without worrying about grammar, spelling or structure.</li>
<li>Discuss the benefits of free writing, such as creativity, self-expression, and overcoming writer's block.</li>
<li>Quote who practised free writing as a form of brainstorming.</li>
</ul>
<p>Provide specific prompts to guide their free writing. For example:</p>
<ul>
<li>Write about a place you've never been but would love to visit.</li>
<li>Create a story using the following three words: adventure, mystery, laughter.</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; font-weight:600;">Explanation</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">
<ul>
<li>Allow students to share their free writing with the class, emphasising the importance of creativity and expressing oneself without fear of judgment.</li>
<li>Discuss the diversity of ideas and writing styles within the class.</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; font-weight:600;">Elaboration</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">
<ul>
<li>Ask students to exchange their free writing. Encourage constructive feedback and suggestions for improvement.</li>
<li>Based on peer feedback, students can revise and improve their initial free writing.</li>
<li>Emphasise the importance of the writing process and continuous improvement.</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; font-weight:600;">Evaluation</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">
<ul>
<li>Have students reflect on the experience of free writing. Ask them to consider what they enjoyed, any challenges they faced, and how they can apply the practice in their future writing.</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>
</table>
