<p><strong>Students will be able to:</strong></p><ul><li>explain the need for standard form/scientific notation (U).</li><li>utilise scientific notation in measurements (A).</li></ul>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>

<p>Whiteboard, duster, markers or chalk, small square, measuring tape or ruler.</p>

<h4>3. Motivation Activity (6 minutes)</h4>

<p>The teacher measures the dimensions of the whiteboard with the help of a student, calculates its area, and compares it with the area of a small square.</p>

<h4>4. Universe's Distances and Sizes (10 minutes)</h4>

<ul><li>Draw a large dot on the paper, labelling it "Earth."</li><li>Explain the concept of astronomical units (AU) and light-years (LY) as units of measurement for distances in space.</li><li>Discuss the immense distances between stars, galaxies, and other celestial bodies.</li></ul>

<h4>5. Scientific Notation Conversion (20 minutes)</h4>

<ul><li>Explain the concept of scientific notation.</li><li>Ask students to convert numbers into scientific notation.</li><li>Discuss and compare the sizes of objects in scientific notation.</li></ul>

<h4>6. Assessment (5 minutes)</h4>

<p>Challenge students to come up with their own examples of large numbers and convert them into scientific notation.</p>

<h4>7. Resources</h4>
