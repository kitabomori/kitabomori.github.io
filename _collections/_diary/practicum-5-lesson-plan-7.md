<h4>1. Student's Learning Outcomes</h4>
<p><strong>Students will be able to:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>I. Explain the need for Standard Form/ Scientific Notation (U).</li>
  <li>II. Utilise scientific notation in measurements (A).</li>
</ul>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>
<p>Whiteboard, duster, markers or chalk. small square, measuring tape or ruler</p>

<h4>3. Motivation Activity (6 Minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>The teacher will measure the dimensions of the whiteboard with the help of the student.</li>
  <li>Calculate the area of the whiteboard and compare it with the area of a small square.</li>
</ul>

<h4>4. Universe's Distances and Sizes (10 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Draw a large dot on the paper, labelling it "Earth."</li>
  <li>Explain the concept of astronomical units (AU) and light-years (LY) as units of measurement for distances in space.</li>
  <li>Discuss the immense distances between stars, galaxies, and other celestial bodies.</li>
</ul>

<h4>5. Scientific Notation Conversion (20 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Explain the concept of Scientific Notation.</li>
  <li>Ask students to convert these numbers into scientific notation.</li>
  <li>Discuss and compare the sizes of the objects in scientific notation.</li>
</ul>

<h4>6. Assessment (5 Minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Challenge students to come up with their own examples of large numbers</li>
  <li>Have them convert these numbers into scientific notation.</li>
</ul>

</ul>
