<h4>2. Required Material</h4>
<p>Board (white/black), chalk/marker, duster</p>

<h4>3. Introduction</h4>
<p>The teacher will begin the lesson by asking students what they think it means to be truthful. He will discuss the importance of truthfulness in our daily lives, and the consequences of lying or being dishonest by sharing a personal anecdote or story that illustrates the value of truthfulness.</p>

<h4>4. Thinking Activity</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Divide students into small groups and provide a scenario where honesty is tested, such as finding money on the ground or witnessing a friend cheat on a test.</li>
  <li>Ask the groups to discuss and present how they would respond to the situation, emphasizing the importance of truthfulness.</li>
  <li>Encourage classmates to share their thoughts and opinions about the scenarios presented.</li>
</ul>

<h4>5. Assessment</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Observe and evaluate students' speaking and listening skills during the presentation and thinking activities.</li>
  <li>Provide feedback and guidance to help students improve their language skills.</li>
</ul>
