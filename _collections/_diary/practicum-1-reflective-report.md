<div style="text-align:right; font-size:0.85rem; color:#666; margin:0 0 1rem 0;">(January 20, 2024)</div>

<p>The third semester of B.Ed.-III started in October 2021. With other courses, Practicum-I was also part of the semester. The practicum course is unique in that it aims to provide prospective teachers with experience by exposing them to the school environment through weekly visits. It also has specific objectives, such as identifying connections between teaching theory and practice. The other rationales are: to collaborate with peers, cooperating teachers, school staff, and the practicum supervisor; and to develop professional relationships by observing elements of the school from the gatekeeper to the principal. Most of the lot was to invite, accept and utilise feedback from the cooperating teacher, supervisor and peers for learning.</p>

<p>To achieve the objectives, the practicum was completed in 16 weeks. The supervisor allocated the first two weeks to discuss what the practicum is all about, what we (student teachers) are going to do in the coming weeks and to teach us how to write a good reflection. Then, for the following 10 weeks, we visited the Government X School in Old Sukkur, each time with an observation sheet listing a different objective to be achieved. Every visit added small chunks to my knowledge, and the supervisor asked every student to write a reflection to confirm whether they were learning and showing any signs of improvement. At the same time, the remaining three weeks were dedicated to reflecting on and presenting the learnings in three seminar presentations. And the last part of the lot was to write a reflective report summarising everything that happened in the semester.</p>

<p>School is not just a building; it’s a place where pupils get an education. Education is only possible when a good environment is provided. A good environment includes a workforce comprising teachers and management. Still, good infrastructure is vital, including buildings with quality classrooms, labs, a library, a cafe, washrooms, a playing area, and every basic facility, from clean drinking water to first aid. By observing the Government X School in Old Sukkur from different perspectives, I learned about various elements of a school: how the different sections of the school are interlinked, and that chain is vital for a school to perform productively. Apart from this, the school visits and the process of reflecting enabled me to focus on certain areas, such as:</p>

<ol><li>The role of management in running a school smoothly.</li><li>The use of available resources for the learning of students.</li><li>How essential is the movement of a teacher in the classroom?</li><li>What encourages a student to participate in the class?</li><li>The importance of lesson planning.</li><li>Which tactics can be used to check whether students are attentive or not? E.g. questioning, asking for opinions, suggestions and on-the-spot reflections, etc.</li><li>The value of giving clear verbal instructions.</li><li>Ensuring the involvement of all the students in class in the learning process.</li><li>Why is it so handy to know the psyche of students?</li><li>The vitality of policy documents for a school to deal with any matters of misconduct impartially.</li><li>I went through the process of evaluating the performance of a teacher and found out that a teacher should have expertise in certain areas.</li><li>I learnt about the responsibility of a teacher, where one should have an idea of what's going on in the life of a student, whether they are happy or bullied.</li></ol>

<p>In previous semesters, I learned about educational theories, how to manage a classroom, which teaching methods to implement, and whether a student- or teacher-centred classroom would be ideal for teaching in the 21st century. The practicum allowed me to test and verify theories of education in the field. It helped me tremendously by enabling me to compare the theories with what is happening in schools.</p>

<p>Throughout the allocated weeks, I met with every possible person available at the school. At the end of the day, this experience of conversing and reflecting on the practicum's objectives, based on the observation sheet, helped me achieve them. When someone achieves their goals, they feel satisfied with the work they are doing, and that gives them the confidence to go beyond the set boundaries. In the first phase of five weeks, when things were shaky, unclear and incomprehensible, I felt the practicum was useless practice. The second five weeks were when I understood the rationale behind the practicum-I course, and the reasons became clear: one has to develop certain skills and qualities by observing the cooperating teachers and the school. So, from then on, I felt quite satisfied with the rest of the semester. I enjoyed sharing what I had come across with fellows and tried to learn from them as well.</p>

<p>In reverse-chronological order, the practicum is a huge success for me, as I am meeting the set targets. The seminars helped me give deep thought to what I was doing throughout the school visit. Looking back, especially during the practicum's initial phase, I was going nowhere. It was the time when I thought this whole practicum course was a mere time pass and a useless thing. As time went on, things became clear, and I understood the objectives of the practicum. Presenting the experience to the whole class gave me the indication that I had observed the required things and learned from them.</p>

<p>The whole semester was a tremendous opportunity to develop certain skills and to reveal some of the strengths and shortcomings in my personality. I realised I can remain focused on the task at hand and handle pressure. The other thing is, when responsibility is given to me, I believe I can offer my best by being cooperative and remaining impartial whenever there is a need of mine. My colleagues shared the areas of concern in my personality with me. It seemed strange to me to present the fact or opinion that I do not value or listen to others' opinions. On the other hand, I do not celebrate small achievements, but the accumulation of small memories and little successes leads to big events.</p>

<p>The skills which I developed during my practicum are:</p>

<ol><li>A polite manner of communicating with professionals, peers and the people around you.</li><li>Enhance my ability to question the things which are not moving on the track.</li><li>Writing a better reflection than an average one.</li><li>Improved presenting data and learnings in a few possible words during a presentation.</li><li>The value of doing things on time, etc.</li><li>Arranging data, saving and storing for later use in times of need, in online platforms like Google Drive, etc.</li><li>A bit of my spoken and written English improved.</li></ol>

<p>There is always room for improvement, and as a prospective teacher, I believe one should be an all-rounder. Below are the areas in which I want to develop and improve:</p>

<ol><li>To improve my oral communication and further improve my writing.</li><li>Making a good presentation using the available resources.</li><li>The ability to work in a team and leadership skills.</li></ol>

<p>There is no use becoming a teacher who is not intrinsically motivated. One thing I believe was missing throughout the practicum was the motivation and passion that students and teachers would have developed by seeing the devotion of cooperating teachers to their profession of teaching at the respective school. The school which we visited was a place where anyone with high hopes would end up in deep despair. Next time, when I get a chance to visit the school, I will be looking to develop this by asking the cooperating teacher why they are teaching. The rest of the course's tactics and techniques were fine, as I went through a process of getting to know the school's dimensions.</p>
