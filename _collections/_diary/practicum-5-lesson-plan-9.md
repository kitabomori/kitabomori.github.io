<p><strong>Students will be able to:</strong></p><ul><li>construct angles using a ruler and compass.</li><li>demonstrate proficiency in geometric construction techniques.</li></ul>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>

<p>Whiteboard, duster, markers or chalk, compass, ruler.</p>

<h4>3. Motivation Activity (5 minutes)</h4>

<p>Imagine you are new in Karachi, in Saddar, and want to meet someone in Gulshan-e-Hadeed. You don't know the directions &mdash; what would you do?</p>

<h4>4. Introductory Activity (10 minutes)</h4>

<p>Introduce the concept of angles in determining directions. Differentiate between azimuths and bearings as ways to measure directions and angles in land surveying and navigation.</p>

<p style="font-style:italic; color:#666;">[A figure illustrating azimuths and bearings was included in the original document as an image and has not been carried over here.]</p>

<h4>5. Activity 1: Construction of Angles (10 minutes)</h4>

<p>Demonstrate how to construct a specific angle (e.g., 60 degrees) using a scale and compass, teach students to bisect an angle, and guide them step-by-step to construct angles that are multiples of 15 (30, 45, 60, etc.).</p>

<h4>6. Assessment Activity (5 minutes)</h4>

<p>Provide each student with a different angle measure to construct independently. Observe their construction process and assess the accuracy of their angles.</p>
