<h4>1. Students' Learning Outcomes</h4>
<p><strong>Students will be able to:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>I. Construct angles using a ruler and compass.</li>
  <li>II. Demonstrate proficiency in geometric construction techniques.</li>
</ul>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>
<p>Whiteboard, duster, markers or chalk, compass, ruler</p>

<h4>3. Motivation Activity (5 Minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Imagine you are new in Karachi, Saddar and want to meet someone at Gulshan Hadeed.</li>
  <li>You don't know the directions, what would you do then?</li>
</ul>

<h4>4. Introductory Activity (10 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Introduce the concept of angles in determining directions.</li>
  <li>Differentiate between Azimuths and Bearings, ways to measure directions and angles in land surveying and navigation using the figure. 1.</li>
</ul>

<h4>5. Activity 1: Construction of Angles (10 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Demonstrate how to construct a specific angle (e.g., 60 degrees) using a scale and compass and teach the students to bisect an angle</li>
  <li>Guide students step-by-step to construct angles that are multiples of 15, i.e. 30, 45, 60, etc.</li>
</ul>

<h4>6. Assessment Activity (5 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Provide each student with a different angle measure to construct independently.</li>
  <li>Observe their construction process and assess the accuracy of their angles.</li>
</ul>
