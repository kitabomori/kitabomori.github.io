<p><em>Reflection on Lessons Six and Seven</em></p>

<p>The important aspects of a class and teaching that caught my attention on the visit on March 1, 2023, to Government High School No. 1, Sukkur are:</p>

<ul><li>I haven't been able to create a positive relationship with students and a learning environment.</li><li>The role of the teacher is not just to teach but to give hope and reason to students to learn.</li><li>There is a lack of curiosity to learn from the student side.</li></ul>

<p>Firstly, after five interactions, I have been struggling to build a positive relationship with my students and foster a conducive learning environment, which has made me question how effective my teaching is. There could be several reasons for that. I might lack empathy or understanding for the students. Maybe I cannot communicate effectively with the students because I am using a third language to convey my message. But it is of utmost importance to have a positive relationship with students. According to Hamre &amp; Pianta (2006), positive relationships with students can lead to improved academic performance, increased motivation and greater engagement in learning. Students who have supportive relationships with their teachers tend to feel safer and improve academically, and vice versa.</p>

<p>Secondly, the students look aimless and hopeless about improving their current situation in terms of what they know. Therefore, the role of a teacher is not just to impart knowledge, but also to provide hope and inspire the students to learn. It's not enough to teach the content. One must also help students understand the relevance of what they are learning and how it can benefit them in the future. Therefore, I need to find ways to inspire and motivate my students to be more engaged in learning.</p>

<p>Finally, I have noticed a lack of curiosity among my students to learn. This is concerning, as it may hinder their academic progress and personal development. I think building positive relationships with students and getting to know them better by engaging in brief conversations about their interests and discussing ideas and concepts beyond the classroom will make them more curious to ask questions and more active in class.</p>

<p>In conclusion, as a teacher, I need to focus not only on imparting knowledge but also on creating a positive learning environment that fosters curiosity, motivation and eagerness to learn. I believe that by building positive relationships with my students and making learning relevant, I can help them achieve their full potential.</p>
