<p style="font-size:0.85rem; color:#666; text-align:left;"><strong>Reflection written on:</strong> Wednesday, October 25, 2023</p>

<h3>Lesson V Reflection</h3>

<p>The usual day-to-day lesson plan of our teaching practice seems outdated to me, as it focuses only on the banking model of education, where the more knowledgeable person (teacher) deposits a chunk of information in students' minds without creating knowledge or accounting for the factors that influence the learning process. In one of the counselling and guidance courses here at Sukkur IBA University, I came to understand the importance of counselling. So today I was questioning whether my students need any kind of counseling or a bit of help here and there that could help them to act as active learners and to be ready to learn something new. So before taking the class of 8th-Daisy, I sat with a couple of students and had a conversation in the break. I was looking at what could be the possible reasons that are barriers to the learning process of students. In that sense today was sort of another day for me at the field as I came to know about the psychology of students and the stresses they are having.</p>

<p>Among the students whom I talked to today, one belongs to the minority Hindu community. The conversation with him revealed that the school management is not concerned about the religious festivities specially of the minority students. This particular student was worried about his upcoming exams from next week and not having a holiday before the Diwali celebration. My question is; would he be able to enjoy Diwali as he would spend his time preparing for exams? At the same time he was saying that when there is any religious event of the Muslim community there used to be holidays and the exam or events are arranged accordingly. This all was observed by an 8th grader. He was feeling segregated and excluded not only in the class but in the whole school and community. Secondly, there was another student who was counting only two true friends among the other 32 students in the class. These are just two examples and there would be other problems of students as well which need to be catered. I am sure that this unequal treatment is putting a lot of stress on him and such students. In a stressed situation it is next to impossible to pay attention to what is being taught in the classroom.</p>

<p>In order to help the students to cope with such issues of psychological and socialization there is a need for a teacher to be a counselor. The teacher counselor would listen and help students to share their concerns and doubts in order to remain free from stress and to focus on what is going on in the classroom.</p>
