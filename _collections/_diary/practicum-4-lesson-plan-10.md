<h4>4. 5E Plan</h4>

<table style="border-collapse:collapse; margin:1rem 0; font-size:0.95rem; width:100%;">
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; font-weight:600;">Engagement</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">
<ul>
<li>Start the course with a quick review of the previous class on free writing.</li>
<li>Ask students to share their thoughts on the concept of free writing based on the previous lesson.</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>

<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; font-weight:600;">Exploration</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">
<ul>
<li>Remind students of the definition of free writing and its benefits.</li>
<li>Introduce additional prompts for free writing and encourage students to explore different texts or topics.</li>
</ul>

<p>Provide specific prompts to guide their free writing. For example:</p>

<ul>
<li>Describe a moment that changed your mind.</li>
<li>Write a conversation between two people with opposing viewpoints.</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>

<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; font-weight:600;">Explanation</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">
<ul>
<li>Facilitate a class discussion about the value of sharing and receiving feedback.</li>
<li>Incorporate the idea of revision and refinement into the writing process.</li>
<li>Discuss specific areas of writing that could be improved, such as word choice, sentence structure, and clarity of expression.</li>
<li>Model the process of revising a piece of writing based on the feedback received.</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>

<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; font-weight:600;">Elaboration</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">
<ul>
<li>Have students revise one of their earlier free writing pieces or use one of the new prompts.</li>
<li>In pairs or small groups, students edit their writing and provide constructive feedback.</li>
<li>Encourage students to focus on specific aspects of writing discussed in class, such as adding emotional detail and improving sentence structure.</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>

<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px; font-weight:600;">Evaluation</td></tr>
<tr><td style="border:1px solid #ddd; padding:8px;">
<ul>
<li>Ask each student to reflect on the feedback they received and the process of making changes to their writing.</li>
<li>Facilitate a class discussion about the challenges and successes they experienced during revision.</li>
</ul>
</td></tr>
</table>
