<p><strong>Students will be able to:</strong></p><ul><li>classify different types of numbers in sets (U).</li><li>demonstrate that there exist infinitely many numbers between two number sets (A).</li></ul>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>

<p>Whiteboard, duster, markers or chalk.</p>

<h4>3. Motivation Activity (5 minutes)</h4>

<p>Begin with a quick game of "guess the number of numbers." Write a series of numbers on the board and ask how many numbers there are between any two of them.</p>

<h4>4. Introductory Activity (15 minutes)</h4>

<ul><li>Introduce the concept of number sets, explaining that numbers can be grouped into categories based on their properties.</li><li>Draw a Venn diagram on the board, labelling the circles with the sets: Natural, Whole, Integers, Rational, and Irrational Numbers.</li><li>Briefly explain each set and provide examples.</li></ul>

<h4>5. Activity 1 (10 minutes)</h4>

<p>Discuss and correct any misconceptions, and ask students to identify patterns they notice between the different number sets.</p>

<h4>6. Assessment Activity (5 minutes)</h4>

<p>Students solve problems individually, demonstrating their ability to classify and perform operations with number sets.</p>

<h4>7. Resources</h4>

<p>Mathematics 9th book.</p>
