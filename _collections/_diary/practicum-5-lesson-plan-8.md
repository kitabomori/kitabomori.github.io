<h4>1. Students' Learning Outcomes</h4>
<p><strong>Students will be able to:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>I. Classify different types of numbers in sets(U).</li>
  <li>II. Demonstrate that there exist infinitely many numbers between two number sets (A)</li>
</ul>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>
<p>Whiteboard, duster, markers or chalk.</p>

<h4>3. Motivation Activity (5 Minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Begin with a quick game of "guess the number of numbers".</li>
  <li>Write down a series of numbers on the board.</li>
  <li>Ask, how many numbers are there between any two numbers?</li>
</ul>

<h4>4. Introductory Activity (15 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Introduce the concept of number sets by explaining that numbers can be grouped into different categories based on their properties.</li>
  <li>Draw a Venn diagram on the board, labelling the circles with the following sets: Natural, Whole, Integers, Rational, and Irrational Numbers.</li>
  <li>Briefly explain each set and provide examples.</li>
</ul>

<h4>5. Activity 1 (10 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Discuss and correct any misconceptions.</li>
  <li>Ask students to identify any patterns they notice between the different number sets.</li>
</ul>

<h4>6. Assessment Activity (5 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Students will solve the problems individually, demonstrating their ability to classify and perform operations with number sets.</li>
</ul>

<h4>7. Resources</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Mathematics 9th book</li>
</ul>
