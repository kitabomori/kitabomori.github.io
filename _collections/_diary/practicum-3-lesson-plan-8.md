<h4>2. Required Material</h4>
<p>Board (white/black), chalk/marker, duster</p>

<table style="border-collapse:collapse; margin:1rem 0; font-size:0.95rem; width:100%; border:1px solid #333;">
  <tr>
    <th style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; background:#f5f5f5; text-align:center; font-weight:600;">Engagement</th>
    <td style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; vertical-align:top;">
      <ul>
        <li>Start the lesson by asking the students if they have ever heard of</li>
        <ol>
          <li>Acute angle</li>
          <li>Obtuse angle</li>
          <li>Right angle</li>
          <li>Straight angle</li>
          <li>Complementary angle</li>
          <li>Supplementary angle</li>
        </ol>
        <li>Write the word "Angle" on the board and ask the students to brainstorm different types of angles they know.</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <th style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; background:#f5f5f5; text-align:center; font-weight:600;">Exploration</th>
    <td style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; vertical-align:top;">
      <ul>
        <li>Distribute angle measuring tools (protractors) to each student.</li>
        <li>Have the students explore how to use the angle measuring tool by measuring the angles on their worksheets or flashcards.</li>
        <li>Assist the students in measuring angles, reminding them of how to properly align the protractor and where to read the measurements.</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <th style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; background:#f5f5f5; text-align:center; font-weight:600;">Explanation</th>
    <td style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; vertical-align:top;">
      <ul>
        <li>Review the measurements of each angle with the students and then introduce them to the different types of angles. Explain the characteristics and definitions of each type:</li>
        <ul>
          <li>Acute angle</li>
          <li>Right angle</li>
          <li>Obtuse angle</li>
          <li>Straight angle</li>
        </ul>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <th style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; background:#f5f5f5; text-align:center; font-weight:600;">Elaboration</th>
    <td style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; vertical-align:top;">
      <ul>
        <li>Provide students with real-world scenarios where they can identify different types of angles, such as the angle formed between the hands on a clock or the angle formed by a street intersection.</li>
        <li>Have students work in pairs or small groups to create their own scenarios and challenge each other to identify the types of angles within them.</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <th style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; background:#f5f5f5; text-align:center; font-weight:600;">Evaluation</th>
    <td style="padding:6px 12px; border:1px solid #333; vertical-align:top;">
      <ul>
        <li>Distribute a short quiz or worksheet to assess the students' understanding of the different types of angles.</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
</table>
