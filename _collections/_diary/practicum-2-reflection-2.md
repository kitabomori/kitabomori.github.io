<p>We visited the Government Primary School on March 3rd, 2022. When we arrived at the classroom, the cooperating teacher was already in the middle of a math lesson. We waited for her to complete her lesson. As the group had decided, it was my turn to deliver the lesson plan. I had mentally prepared myself to deliver the lesson, but I was not fully prepared. The class had 25 students, but only 14 attended. Initially, I tried to engage the students by inquiring about their daily routine. Then, while distributing some apples, I divided them into parts and asked the students questions. This activity was designed to draw the students' attention to the topic "fractions." The class was over in the blink of an eye. During the lesson, the co-teachers observed my delivery and provided valuable feedback.</p>

<p>The co-teachers' feedback will be extremely beneficial in improving my teaching style and the way I interact with students, as they identified weaknesses in my lesson delivery. As the co-teachers correctly pointed out, a teacher needs to know everything about the students, from their names to their backgrounds to their understanding, skills, learning styles, and areas where they are weak. It was my first real experience as a teacher at a school. I was unaware of the aforementioned areas of the students' knowledge, which pushed me back from achieving the lesson's objectives, and I felt alienated in the class, which knew nothing about the subject. The second concerning aspect was that I overloaded the student with content knowledge without allowing adequate time for the student to digest the information. At the same time, I deviated from the main topic, which made the lesson difficult for the students. Finally, due to time constraints, I was unable to assess the students to determine what they had learned properly.</p>

<p>As I identified weaknesses in my lesson plan delivery, this first lesson would be an opportunity for me to learn and improve. My energy, voice, and movement in class were all appropriate, but my performance in other areas was below average. As a result, this and future teaching and observation of co-teachers' lesson delivery will assist me.</p>
