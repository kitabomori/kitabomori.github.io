<h4>1. General Objectives</h4>

<p>Students will be able to:</p>

<ul><li>develop mathematical curiosity when solving problems.</li><li>become confident in using mathematics to analyse and solve problems both in school and in real-life situations.</li><li>develop the knowledge, skills, and attitudes necessary to pursue further studies in mathematics.</li></ul>

<h4>2. Specific Objective (SLOs)</h4>

<p>Students will be able to plot an irrational number on the real number line.</p>

<h4>3. Instructional Aids</h4>

<p>Board, chalk/board marker, textbook, compass, protractor, scale, and pencil.</p>

<h4>4. Motivational Activity</h4>

<p>The teacher will draw/show a unit right-angle triangle, then, for the length of the hypotenuse, write the value of the irrational √2 = 1.41421&hellip; Next, the teacher will ask different questions of the students, such as:</p>

<ul><li>Can you guess what this number could be?</li><li>Is it a rational number or irrational?</li><li>Can you represent this number on the number line?</li></ul>

<p>To create interest in the students to learn something new, the teacher will announce, at the end of this questioning time, that we are going to learn to represent “an irrational number on the number line.”</p>

<h4>5. Previous Knowledge</h4>

<p>Students are aware of the number system, which contains rational and irrational numbers. At the same time, they also know about the Pythagoras theorem (c = √(a²+b²)) and constructing a perpendicular line using a compass or protractor, as these topics are part of grade 6 and 7 mathematics. In an actual class, the teacher would try to teach the above topics if the students are unable to recall them.</p>

<h4>6. Demonstration</h4>

<p>The teacher will teach the students by demonstrating how to represent an irrational number, such as √2 and √5, on the number line using a scale, protractor, and compass on the blackboard.</p>

<h4>7. Assessment</h4>

<p>At the end, the teacher would give students the number √5 to plot on the number line, to check whether they had understood the concept.</p>
