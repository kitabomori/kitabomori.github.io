<h4>1. General Objectives</h4>
<p>Students will be able to:</p>
<ul>
  <li>develop mathematical curiosity when solving problems.</li>
  <li>become confident in using mathematics to analyse and solve problems both in school and in real-life situations.</li>
  <li>develop the knowledge, skills, and attitudes necessary to pursue further studies in mathematics.</li>
</ul>

<h4>2. Specific Objective (SLOs)</h4>
<p>Students will be able to:</p>
<ul>
  <li>plot an irrational number on the real number line.</li>
</ul>

<h4>3. Instructional Aids</h4>
<p>Board, chalks/board marker, textbook, compass, protector, scale, and pencil.</p>

<h4>4. Motivational Activity</h4>
<p>The teacher will draw/show a unit right-angle triangle. Then for the length of hypotenuse will write the value of the irrational √2 = 1.41421 35623 73095 04880 16887 24209 69807 85696 71875 37694 80731 76679 73799...</p>
<p>Next, the teacher will ask different questions from the student such as</p>
<ul>
  <li>Can you guess what could this number be?</li>
  <li>Is it a rational number or irrational?</li>
  <li>Can you represent this number on the number line?</li>
</ul>
<p>To create interest in the students to learn something new and at the end of this questioning time, the teacher will announce today that we are going to learn to represent "an irrational number on the number line".</p>

<h4>5. Previous Knowledge</h4>
<p>Students are aware of the number system, which contains rational and irrational numbers. At the same time, they also know about the Pythagoras theorem (c =√a²+b²) and constructing a perpendicular line using the compress or protector, because these topics are part of grades 6th and 7th mathematics.</p>
<p>In an actual class, the teacher would try to teach the above topics if the students are unable to recall them.</p>

<h4>6. Demonstration</h4>
<p>The teacher will teach the student by demonstrating how to represent an irrational, such as √2 and √5 on the number line using a scale, protector, and compass on the blackboard.</p>

<h4>7. Assessment</h4>
<p>At the end, the teacher would give the number √5 students to plot on the number line to check whether the students have understood the concept or not.</p>
