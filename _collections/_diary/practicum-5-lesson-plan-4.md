<h4>1. Student Learning Objectives</h4>

<p><strong>Students will be able to:</strong></p><ul><li>analyse the characteristics of diary writing.</li><li>write a diary entry using appropriate language and structure.</li></ul>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>

<p>Board, chalk, duster, copies of sample diary entries.</p>

<h4>3. Motivational Activity (5 minutes)</h4>

<p>Read aloud an excerpt from a famous diary, such as Anne Frank's. Ask students what they find interesting about diary writing and why they think people keep diaries.</p>

<h4>4. Introductory Activity (5 minutes)</h4>

<p>Discuss the purpose of diary writing. Brainstorm possible topics or events one might write about in a diary.</p>

<h4>5. Characteristics of Diary Writing (10 minutes)</h4>

<p>Present a list of characteristics of diary writing on the board (first-person perspective, informal language, chronological order, personal reflections) and explain how they contribute to the overall style and tone of diary writing.</p>

<h4>6. Assessment Activity: Writing a Diary Entry (15 minutes)</h4>

<p>Provide students with a scenario (e.g., "Imagine a new teacher has joined your school. Write a diary entry about how he/she teaches or treats you on the very first day") and instruct them to use the characteristics of diary writing discussed earlier.</p>

<h4>7. Homework (Optional)</h4>

<p>Students write a diary entry about a recent event or experience in their lives.</p>

<h4>8. Reference</h4>

<p>Excerpt from Anne Frank's diary.</p>
