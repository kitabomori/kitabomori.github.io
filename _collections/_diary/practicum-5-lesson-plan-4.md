<h4>1. Student Learning Outcomes</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Analyse the characteristics of diary writing.</li>
  <li>Write a diary entry using appropriate language and structure.</li>
</ul>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>
<p>Board, chalk, duster, copies of sample diary entries</p>

<h4>3. Motivational Activity (5 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Read aloud an excerpt from a famous diary, such as Anne Frank's diary.</li>
  <li>Ask students what they find interesting about diary writing and why they think people keep diaries</li>
</ul>

<h4>4. Introductory Activity (5 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Discuss the purpose of writing a diary.</li>
  <li>Brainstorm the possible topics or events that one might write about in a diary.</li>
</ul>

<h4>5. Characteristics of Diary Writing (10 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Present a list of characteristics of diary writing on the board (e.g., first-person perspective, informal language, chronological order, personal reflections).</li>
  <li>Explain how they contribute to the overall style and tone of the diary writing.</li>
</ul>

<h4>6. Assessment Activity: Writing Diary Entry (15 minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Provide students with a scenario (e.g., "Imagine a new teacher has joined your school. Write a diary entry about how he/she teaches or treats you on the very first day").</li>
  <li>Instruct students to use the characteristics of diary writing discussed earlier.</li>
</ul>

<h4>7. Homework (Optional)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Assign students to write a diary entry about a recent event or experience in their lives.</li>
</ul>

<h4>8. Reference</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Excerpt from Anne Frank's diary</li>
</ul>
