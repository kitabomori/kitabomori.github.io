<h4>1. Students' Learning Outcomes</h4>
<p><strong>Students will be able to:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>I. Interpret the basics of sound amplification (U)</li>
  <li>II. Identify different sound amplification devices (A)</li>
</ul>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>
<p>Whiteboard, markers, sound amplification devices, microphone, megaphone, loudspeaker</p>

<h4>3. Motivation Activity (5 Minutes)</h4>
<p>Begin with a question:</p>
<ul>
  <li>What is sound amplification, and how do sound amplification devices work?</li>
  <li>What are some examples of sound amplification devices?</li>
</ul>

<h4>4. Demonstration of Amplification (15 Minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Play a short audio clip or music at a low volume and ask the students if they can hear it clearly. Discuss why it may be difficult to hear properly when the volume is low.</li>
  <li>Introducing and explaining the concept of sound amplification</li>
  <li>Discuss real-life scenarios where sound amplification devices are used (e.g., concerts, public speaking, hearing aids for individuals with hearing loss).</li>
</ul>

<h4>5. Group Discussion (10 Minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Hold a classroom discussion and ask the students to share examples of everyday situations where they encounter sound amplification devices.</li>
  <li>Encourage them to explain how these devices help in those situations.</li>
</ul>

<h4>6. Assessment (5 Minutes)</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Have students complete a short quiz or worksheet to assess their understanding of the concepts discussed.</li>
</ul>

<h4>7. Resources</h4>
<ul>
  <li>Science book Grade 8th</li>
</ul>
