<h4>1. Student Learning Objectives</h4>

<p><strong>Students will be able to:</strong></p><ul><li>interpret the basics of sound amplification (U).</li><li>identify different sound amplification devices (A).</li></ul>

<h4>2. Required Material</h4>

<p>Whiteboard, markers, sound amplification devices &mdash; microphone, megaphone, loudspeaker.</p>

<h4>3. Motivation Activity (5 minutes)</h4>

<p>Begin with a question: what is sound amplification, and how do sound amplification devices work? What are some examples?</p>

<h4>4. Demonstration of Amplification (15 minutes)</h4>

<ul><li>Play a short audio clip or music at a low volume, and ask students if they can hear it clearly. Discuss why it may be difficult to hear properly when the volume is low.</li><li>Introduce and explain the concept of sound amplification.</li><li>Discuss real-life scenarios where sound amplification devices are used (concerts, public speaking, hearing aids for individuals with hearing loss).</li></ul>

<h4>5. Group Discussion (10 minutes)</h4>

<p>Hold a classroom discussion and ask students to share examples of everyday situations where they encounter sound amplification devices, and explain how these devices help in those situations.</p>

<h4>6. Assessment (5 minutes)</h4>

<p>Have students complete a short quiz or worksheet to assess their understanding of the concepts discussed.</p>

<h4>7. Resources</h4>

<p>Science book Grade 8th.</p>
