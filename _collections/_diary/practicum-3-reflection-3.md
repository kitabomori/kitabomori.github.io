<p style="text-align:right; font-size:0.85rem; color:#666; margin:0 0 1rem 0;">(February 22, 2023)</p>

<p><em>Reflection on Lessons Four and Five</em></p>

<p>As I reflect on the recent lessons I taught on Feb 22, I observed that:</p>

<ul><li>Students desired more hands-on learning/activities.</li><li>It was a challenge for me to provide attention to each student.</li><li>I did not get the response I had hoped for from the students.</li></ul>

<p>Firstly, it became evident that students wanted more hands-on activities to engage with the content. This indicates that traditional teaching methods may not be enough for some students to retain information. They were not as engaged or motivated during the lesson. Thus, I need to think more creatively and design more interactive, engaging activities that cater to students' diverse learning styles. The hands-on approach increases students' academic achievement and understanding of scientific concepts by using objects, which may make abstract knowledge more concrete and clearer. Through a hands-on approach, students can engage with real-life illustrations and observe the effects of changes in different variables (O. Ekwueme et al., 2015).</p>

<p>Secondly, I noticed that it is challenging to pay attention to individual needs. Given the large class size, it may be difficult to address each student's specific learning needs. However, it is crucial to identify struggling students and offer them support where possible. I can achieve this by providing an additional lesson to supplement the class lessons.</p>

<p>Lastly, I did not get the response I was expecting from the students. This could be due to various factors, including the difficulty level of the content or the teaching method I used. I need to examine the teaching methods I have been using and evaluate their effectiveness. I can also use feedback from my co-teacher to refine the lesson plan and teaching approach.</p>

<p>Moving forward, I plan to address these observations in several ways. I will develop more hands-on activities, such as small-group tasks related to the content, to help students work together and learn from each other. Secondly, I will be more attentive and encourage students to reach out if they are struggling with any aspect of the class. Lastly, I will keep an open mind and be willing to adapt my teaching methods to best serve my students' needs.</p>
