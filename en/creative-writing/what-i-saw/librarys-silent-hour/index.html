A Library's Silent Hour

A small public library in the city, mid-afternoon. About fifteen people were there. Some were students, some older. An elderly man read a newspaper through thick glasses. A young woman with an infant in her lap read a novel — the baby was asleep and she kept reading.

There was something quietly reassuring about it: in spite of the digital world pressing in from all sides, some people still chose to sit with paper. The library was not silent, but it was not noisy either. It had that particular breathing quality that only exists when many people are reading in the same place at the same time.
