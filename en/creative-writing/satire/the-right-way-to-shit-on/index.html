The Right Way to Shit on...

Click for audio on YouTube

Every day, there is a cry from various segments and walks of life. Children want freedom from the beady eyes of parents. Students are demanding their right to quality education and freedom of expression at educational institutions. And women blaming their male counterparts for being dominant and oppressors for ages, etc. At times, congregations discuss plans to break the shackles of oppression, and male treatment catches a lot of attention

But recently, I came across something peculiar, which gave me goosebumps. Toilets, yes, toilets, they were and are on strike. You would say I am insane. This is true, and before any disclosure, why they are on the roads, let me tell you about the caste and creed of toilets. Mostly, we have two types of toilets in this country. One is called the traditional, or Indian, and the other is known as the western, or English, toilet. The English consider themselves more advanced than the Indians. Well, both help us compute our daily chores, where we excrete the waste we take in as food.

Time and again, toilets have been angry and have expressed their fury. But this time they are shouting, especially the English ones, about how wrong they have been used and want humans to compensate by taking concrete measures. They are also blaming us for being unethical by putting the full weight on the seat in a squatting position, and we don’t know the proper posture for sitting on the toilet seat or what to do after it’s been used. At times, they say that homo-sapiens don’t know how to shit properly. They think that we should be held accountable for our misdeeds.

But wait a minute, English toilets are not blaming the illiterate ones or the ones who have never seen or used them in their life before. They have grudges and complaints about the behaviour of those who deem themselves educated, having studied, or currently studying at higher educational institutions.

To an extent, there is no truth in pointing and blaming humans. We are the best of the creation of the creator, and we are above all. We treat all beings equally. And when we get an education, no one can compete with us in any sense. In primary and secondary education, we learn about ethics and etiquette. At higher secondary and university levels, we practice what we have learned without giving a damn thought to scoring a higher cumulative grade point average. Respecting others’ opinions, accepting things as they are, welcoming everyone and everything with a smile, and giving a hand to those in need are qualities we practise daily. Then, the blame for not knowing how to use a toilet is beyond comprehension. Well, if they are to blame, then we would see them in court.

To make complicated things easier, the toilets had already issued a user manual. To use the English toilet, in particular, one has to lift the lid and sit on it like we used to sit on a chair. Bending forward by making an angle with the seat behind and raising the heels is also recommended for better performance. There is an option of seating adjustment attached to the lid; one can use it according to the bumper. When you are done with the most pressing thing of the moment, you have to use either toilet paper or water to clean the patchy area. At last, the user has to flip the lid back and press the button on the tank to flush the remains of what you have eaten hours before; it can be an unchewed, undigested bean, a conglomeration of hydrochloric acid and several of the gastric and intestinal juices.

Toilets also claim to offer the best places where individuals can find peace of mind. Often claim that the best ideas originate on the toilet seat, where everyone feels free to think about anything. At times, we not only flash the waste but also the tamper, depression and ugly thoughts. Hence, it is a shame to disrespect the toilets which offer the best of their services to inhumane humans.

Satire — any resemblance to specific institutions, policies, ministries, or ceremonies is, regrettably, not entirely coincidental.

05 March 2022
